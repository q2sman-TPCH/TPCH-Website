What Durga Puja means to us

A piece of home, planted in Aotearoa

Durga Puja marks the victory of good over evil — the return of the Goddess Durga to her earthly home, welcomed with ten days of devotion, colour, music and joy. For probasi (diaspora) Bengalis, it carries an extra layer of meaning: the gathering of scattered families, the reassurance that culture travels with us.

At TPCH, we observe Durga Puja over a weekend each year — with morning prayers, anjali, bhog, children's activities, exhibitions of traditional clothing and folk art, and an evening of music and cultural performance open to all.

Food & hospitality

Bhog, Prasad & community meals

Food is central to Durga Puja — from the sacred to the celebratory.

🌸

Prasad & Bhog

Sacred food offered to the Goddess and shared with all who attend. Prasad and Bhog are available to every attendee throughout the weekend — member or guest, this is our offering to all.

🍛

Members' meals

Catered lunches on both days are provided for TPCH members and their families — khichuri, labra, chutney, payesh and the flavours of a proper Puja spread.

Our traditions

The moments that make our Puja

Pushpanjali

পুষ্পাঞ্জলি

The offering of flowers to the Goddess — spoken together as one community, in the language our parents taught us.

Bhog

ভোগ

Sacred food prepared and offered to Durga Maa, then shared as prasad with all present — a moment of equality and grace.

Dhunuchi Naach

ধুনুচি নাচ

The dance of the incense burner — an expression of devotion, joy and community spirit that fills the room with fragrance and energy.

Cultural programme

সাংস্কৃতিক অনুষ্ঠান

An evening of Rabindra Sangeet, Nazrul Geeti, classical dance and community performances — the cultural heart of our celebration.

Bisarjan

বিসর্জন

The farewell to the Goddess — an emotional and beautiful ritual that marks the close of Puja, until we meet again next year.

Sindoor Khela

সিঁদুর খেলা

A joyful tradition where married women exchange sindoor in celebration — a moment of colour, laughter and sisterhood that brings the Puja to its close.
