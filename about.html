Who we are

About Us

A community rooted in Bengali and Eastern Indian heritage, growing together in Tauranga, Aotearoa New Zealand.

How it began

A community that needed a home

Tauranga Probasi Cultural Heritage Incorporated — TPCH — was founded in 2023 by a small group of Bengali and Eastern Indian families who had called the Bay of Plenty home for years, even decades, but felt the pull of something missing: a space where their culture, language and traditions could be celebrated together, and passed on to their children.

The word probasi means diaspora — those who live away from their homeland. For our founding members, it captured exactly who they are: people who carry Bengal in their hearts while building lives in Aotearoa. TPCH was their answer to the question every diaspora community eventually asks: how do we keep our culture alive, here, for the ones who come after us?

Our journey

From a small gathering to a growing community

2023

TPCH is founded

A group of Bengali and Eastern Indian families in Tauranga come together to form Tauranga Probasi Cultural Heritage Incorporated, with a shared vision of cultural preservation and community belonging.

2023

First Durga Puja & Cultural Festival

TPCH organises its first Durga Puja celebration and inaugural Probasi Cultural Festival — bringing together Bengali and Eastern Indian families across the Bay of Plenty for prayers, food, music and cultural performance.

2024

Second Cultural Festival

TPCH's second Probasi Cultural Festival is held, growing in scale and community participation — an evening of Rabindra Sangeet, classical and folk dance, drama and community performances.

2025

Third Cultural Festival & growing programmes

TPCH holds its third Probasi Cultural Festival and expands community programmes — Bengali language activities, children's cultural workshops, Rabindra Jayanti celebrations and inter-cultural outreach.

2026

Coming up — an exciting year ahead

Our fourth Probasi Cultural Festival and Durga Puja on 17–18 October 2026. Planning is already underway!

What drives us

Our values

Preservation

We believe culture is not preserved by accident. It takes intention, effort and community. Everything we do is in service of keeping Bengali and Eastern Indian heritage alive for the next generation.

Belonging

Being probasi — away from home — can be lonely. TPCH exists to ensure that every Bengali and Eastern Indian family in Tauranga has a community where they truly belong.

Openness

Our culture is something we share, not something we keep to ourselves. We welcome all of Tauranga — and all of Aotearoa — to learn, celebrate and connect with us.

The team behind TPCH

Our Executive Committee

TPCH is led by a volunteer executive committee of founding members who give their time, energy and passion to keeping Bengali and Eastern Indian culture alive in Tauranga.

[ Photo: Kajal Mukherjee ] Founding Member
President
Kajal Mukherjee

Kajal came to Tauranga from Calcutta in 1995 and has called it home ever since. A founding member of TPCH, she has served as President since the organisation's inception in 2023, bringing together a community she has always believed deserved a home of its own in the Bay of Plenty.

An engineer by profession, Kajal is at heart a romantic lover of all things Bengali — music, dance, art, cuisine and the language itself. Her deep connection to Rabindra Sangeet and the classical traditions of Bengal is what drives her commitment to ensuring the next generation stays rooted in a culture worth preserving.

"Tauranga is my home. But Bengal lives in my heart — and TPCH is how I keep it alive here."
[ Photo: Pranoy Pal ] Founding Member
General Secretary
Pranoy Pal

Pranoy grew up in Gujarat, born to Bengali parents who ensured that language, culture and tradition remained central to family life — no small feat far from Bengal. He came to Tauranga seven years ago and has made New Zealand home since 2019. An agricultural scientist by profession, Pranoy brings the same careful, methodical dedication to his role at TPCH as he does to his work.

A founding member of TPCH, Pranoy serves as General Secretary — keeping the organisation running with quiet efficiency behind the scenes. His love of Bangla language and Bengali cuisine is at the heart of why this community matters so much to him.

[ Photo: Shreeparna Pal ] Founding Member
Joint Treasurer
Shreeparna Pal

Shreeparna grew up in Gujarat, in a Bengali household where the language, food and traditions of Bengal were always present and cherished. With a background in commerce and finance, she brings exactly the right skills to her role as Joint Treasurer — ensuring TPCH's finances are managed with care and transparency.

A founding member of TPCH, Shreeparna's love of Bangla language and Bengali cuisine is at the heart of why she helped build this organisation from the ground up — a place where the culture she grew up with finds a genuine home in Aotearoa.

[ Photo: Debashis Dutta ] Founding Member
Joint Treasurer
Debashis Dutta

Debashis — known warmly to everyone as Debu — came to New Zealand and settled in Tauranga about twenty years ago. A botanist by profession, he lectures at a local polytechnic, bringing his love of the natural world to the next generation of students.

Debu's story is one of culture carried across two migrations — born in Assam and raised in Shillong, to Bengali parents who kept the language, music and traditions of Bengal alive far from its banks. That same spirit of cultural preservation brought him to TPCH as a founding member, where he serves as Joint Treasurer. An amateur painter and lover of music and the arts, Debu embodies what TPCH is all about — the quiet, joyful determination to keep Bengali culture alive, wherever life may take you.

How we are run

Governance & transparency

Tauranga Probasi Cultural Heritage Incorporated is a registered incorporated society under New Zealand law. We are governed by an elected Executive Committee and operate in accordance with our Constitution and the Incorporated Societies Act 2022.

We are committed to transparency in all our operations — financial, organisational and programmatic — and welcome scrutiny from our members and the wider community.

Constitution

TPCH operates under a formal Constitution adopted at the time of incorporation, setting out our purpose, membership, governance and decision-making processes.
