Who we are

A community rooted in heritage, growing in Aotearoa

Tauranga Probasi Cultural Heritage Incorporated (TPCH) is a community association dedicated to preserving and celebrating Bengali and Eastern Indian cultural heritage in the Tauranga region.

We are immigrants and New Zealanders — people who carry two homes in their hearts. Our work centres on language, music, dance, and the arts, ensuring the next generation stays connected to the culture their families brought with them.

Our flagship event

Probasi Cultural Festival

Our annual celebration of Bengali & Eastern Indian arts — a joyful showcase of music, classical and folk dance, drama, poetry, and cuisine, welcoming all of Tauranga.

Music & song

Rabindra Sangeet, Nazrul Geeti, folk and contemporary Bengali music performed live.

Dance & drama

Classical and folk dance, theatrical presentations celebrating our shared cultural stories.

Arts & cuisine

Visual arts, crafts and food stalls bringing the flavours of Bengal and Eastern India to Tauranga.

What we do

Year-round cultural activities

Beyond the festival, we keep culture alive through programmes throughout the year.

Music & dance workshops

Classical and folk traditions taught by experienced community artists.

Cultural celebrations

Pohela Boishakh and other community occasions marked together through the year.

Youth programmes

Connecting the next generation to their roots through arts, storytelling and culture camps.

Community welfare

Supporting new arrivals and families through settlement, networking and community care.

Inter-cultural outreach

Sharing our heritage with wider Tauranga through public events and school visits.

"We carry our homeland in our hearts — and we are planting its culture into the soil of Aotearoa, for those who come after us."
— TPCH Community, Tauranga
